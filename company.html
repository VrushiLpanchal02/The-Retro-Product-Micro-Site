About the Regency TR-1

The World's First Commercial Transistor Radio - A Technological Revolution

The Birth of Portable Radio

The Regency TR-1 marks a quantum leap in radio technology. For the first time in history, you can carry a high-quality radio receiver in your pocket. Say goodbye to being tethered to electrical outlets or lugging heavy vacuum tube radios.

Developed by Regency engineers in partnership with Texas Instruments and IDEF, the TR-1 harnesses the revolutionary power of the transistor—a tiny semiconductor that amplifies signals without fragile, power-hungry vacuum tubes.

Technical Specifications

  • Frequency Range: 540-1600 kHz (AM Band)
  • Power Output: 100 milliwatts
  • Power Source: One 9-volt battery
  • Battery Life: 20+ hours continuous use
  • Dimensions: 3" × 5" × 1.5"
  • Weight: 12 ounces
  • Construction: Durable Bakelite case
  • Controls: Volume, Tuning, On/Off

How the TR-1 Works

The Transistor Revolution

At the heart of the TR-1 lies a germanium transistor—a remarkable semiconductor device invented just six years ago. This tiny component, no larger than your fingernail, performs the same function as multiple vacuum tubes but uses a fraction of the power and generates minimal heat.

Did you know? The transistor was invented at Bell Laboratories in 1947, and Regency was among the first to commercialize this technology for consumer products.

Superheterodyne Circuit Design

The TR-1 employs a sophisticated superheterodyne circuit that converts incoming radio signals to a standard intermediate frequency for superior selectivity and sensitivity. This means you get clearer reception with less interference from nearby stations.

Precision Engineering

Every component in the TR-1 has been carefully selected and tested. From the high-quality speaker cone to the precision tuning capacitor, no detail was overlooked in creating this revolutionary device.

A Product of Its Time

The 1950s: A World Changing

America in 1954 was entering the golden age of consumer electronics. Television was becoming mainstream, but radio remained the primary source of entertainment and news for millions. The TR-1 arrived at the perfect moment to capitalize on America's growing appetite for portable technology.

Post-War Innovation

The technological breakthroughs that won World War II were rapidly finding civilian applications. The TR-1 represents the peaceful application of military-grade electronics technology for everyday consumer use.

Democratizing Entertainment

For the first time, high-quality radio reception was available to anyone, anywhere. Students could listen to ballgames in the dorm, families could take their music to the backyard, and travelers could enjoy their favorite programs on long journeys.
